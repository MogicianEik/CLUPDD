Welcome To CLUPDD

The Clunio Population Differentiation Database

What are Clunio?

Clunio are a genus of non-biting midges that are found living in the intertidal zone of coasts all over the world. Many species have evolved to synchronize their mating with the lowest tides of the month, during the full moon or new moon, so that they can lay their eggs on the exposed rocks. Clunio marinus has long been a model organism for studying biological clocks, population genetics, and adaptation.

What is CLUPDD?

CLUPDD is a databse that houses and links population variant (SNP) data, the calculated effects that those variants have, and the genes that those variants impact. It is intended to be a tool used by researchers studying the evolution and population genetics of Clunio, or anyone curious to learn more about genetic variation.

What can you do with CLUPDD?

To use the database, head to the Query page or to explore the genome, check out the JBrowse tab. There are currently four ways to search the database. Search by Region to find all SNPs and genes within a region of the genome. Search by Gene will return all SNPs in the gene and its GO terms. Search by SNP to see the genomic features your SNP could affect, and the alternate allele frequencies in each population. Finally, use Advanced Search to query the database directly with MySQL language.
